    Why Toenail Fungus Is a Serious Concern

    One of the most common reasons people search for a podiatrist in Nassau County, NY is because of toenail fungus, also known as onychomycosis. This condition is more than just a cosmetic problem. Toenail fungus causes nails to become thick, brittle, and yellow or brown in color. Over time, the nail can crack, break, or become painful to the touch. In severe cases, toenail infections can spread to other nails or even the surrounding skin, creating discomfort when walking and difficulty wearing shoes.

FAQs (People Also Ask)

A podiatrist is a medical professional specialized in diagnosing, treating, and preventing disorders of the feet, ankles, and lower extremities. They have specific training (in many cases a Doctor of Podiatric Medicine, or DPM) and are often board-certified in foot and ankle care. A general foot doctor may handle minor conditions, but only a certified podiatrist has the knowledge, skills, and tools for complex issues like onychomycosis (fungal toenail infection), structural foot deformities, laser toenail fungus removal, and advanced foot and ankle surgery.
Laser toenail fungus removal (also called nail fungus laser therapy or onychomycosis laser treatment) tends to be more effective than topical creams alone because the laser can penetrate the nail and directly target the fungus underneath. Many patients see improvement of discolored or thickened nails following a few treatments. Oral antifungals can work too, but they may cause side effects and have systemic risks. Laser therapy offers a safer, often more comfortable option with fewer side effects and no need for systemic medication.
Results vary depending on severity of the infection and how much of the nail is affected. You may begin to notice improvement in nail appearance within a few weeks after treatment, but full regrowth of a healthy nail often takes several months. Since nails grow slowly, it may take 6 to 12 months for an infected toenail to grow out completely. During this time, follow-up visits are important to monitor progress, prevent reinfection, and ensure that nail fungus removal stays effective.
